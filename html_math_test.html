Nowak, Werner Georg
Lattice points in a circle: an improved mean-square asymptotics. (English) Zbl 1092.11039
Acta Arith. 113, No. 3, 259-272 (2004).

Summary: Let P(x) denote the lattice point discrepancy (number of integer points minus area) of an origin-centered compact circular disc of radius x. The question for the order of magnitude of P(x), for large x, is known as the Gaussian circle problem. In fact, in this most classic topic, the sharpest known results have been established only quite recently [M. N. Huxley, Proc. Lond. Math. Soc. (3) 87, 591–609 (2003; Zbl 1065.11079)]: Huxley proved that

P(x)≪x 131/416 (logx) 18637/8320 ,(131 416=0·3149⋯)

while K. Soundararajan [Int. Math. Res. Not. 2003, No. 36, 1987–1998; Zbl 1130.11329)] showed that

P(x)≠o((xlogx) 1/4 (loglogx) (3/4)(2 1/3 -1) (logloglogx) -5/8 )·

It is known that P(x)≪x 1/4 in mean-square. More precisely,

∫ 0 X (P(x)) 2 dx=CX 3/2 +Q(X),C≈1·69396·

For this new error term Q(X), the present article provides the refined upper bound

Q(X)≪X(logX) 3/2 loglogX,

thereby improving classic results of Cramér (1922), Landau (1923), Walfisz (1927), and Kátai (1965). The basic idea of the refinement is that most integers are not representable as a sum of two squares, and that those who are (so-called B-numbers) are usually well-spaced – i.e., “B-twins” are still less frequent. This fact permits some extra saving when applying with care a Hilbert-type inequality due to Montgomery and Vaughan.
